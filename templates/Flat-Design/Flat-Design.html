FAQ

Questions, answered flatly

Straight answers, no decoration.

Is flat design just minimalism?+
No. Minimalism reduces for its own sake. Flat design is confidently reductive — it removes depth cues (shadows, bevels, gradients) but keeps bold color, strong typography, and dynamic scale. It's graphic, not empty.
How do hover states work without shadows?+
Through scale transformations (hover:scale-105 on buttons, hover:scale-1.02 on cards), color shifts (darkening or lightening), and color fills (outline buttons filling in). The feedback is immediate and digital, not physical.
Is flat design accessible?+
Yes, when done right. High-contrast color pairing passes WCAG AA. Because we rely on no shadows, focus states use solid ring-2 outlines (ring-blue-500) for clear visibility. Text on color blocks is tested for contrast.
Can I use any color palette?+
Choose a vibrant, confident palette that avoids muddy tones. High contrast is essential. Our default uses Primary Blue (#3B82F6), Emerald (#10B981), and Amber (#F59E0B) on white and gray-100 backgrounds.
