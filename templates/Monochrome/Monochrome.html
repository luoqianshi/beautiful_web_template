The Manifesto

Reduction as
the highest form
of expression.

Maison strips design to its essence: black, white, and typography. There are no gradients to soften edges, no accent colors to distract the eye, no shadows to fabricate depth. Every decision must stand on its own merit — and in that discipline, restraint becomes the ultimate form of expression.

This is the visual language of high-end fashion editorials, architectural monographs, and gallery exhibition catalogs. It commands respect through confidence, using scale, contrast, rhythm, and negative space to create visual drama that color could never achieve.

The result is a system that feels timeless precisely because it refuses to be trendy. It is austere, sophisticated, and unapologetically bold — the kind of design that whispers of permanence in a world addicted to novelty.

The System

Seven principles,
zero compromise.

01 — Palette

Pure monochrome

True black on true white. Gray reserved only for secondary text and dividers. The stark contrast forces deliberate hierarchy at every turn.

02 — Typography

Serif as hero

Playfair Display dominates. The classical serif adds sophistication, editorial weight, and a timeless elegance that sans-serif minimalism cannot.

03 — Scale

Oversized drama

Headlines don't inform — they dominate. Single words fill entire viewport widths, becoming graphic elements rather than mere text.

04 — Lines

Structure by line

Hairlines, thick rules, borders, underlines. Lines create structure without mass — defining edges where shadows and fills would be crude.

05 — Geometry

Sharp precision

Zero border radius everywhere. Perfect ninety-degree corners. The geometry is architectural — Bauhaus meets editorial print design.

06 — Space

Active void

Negative space isn't empty — it's active. Generous margins make black elements more impactful. The page breathes with intention.

The Process

Three movements.

I

Edit ruthlessly

Begin with everything. Remove until only the essential remains. What survives the cut earns its place on the page.

II

Compose with line

Build structure from borders and rules. Let typography breathe within a grid of precise, architectural proportions.

III

Invert for emphasis

Use color inversion — black to white, white to black — to create drama without ever breaking the monochrome rule.

The Value

Why restraint wins.

Timeless by nature

Trends fade; contrast endures. A monochrome system looks as considered in a decade as it does today.

Accessibility built-in

Pure black on white exceeds WCAG AAA at 21:1 contrast. No color, no compromise on legibility.

Instant recognition

Bold serif typography and stark geometry create a brand presence that is unmistakably your own.

Print-perfect fidelity

Designed for editorial print first, the system translates flawlessly to screen with zero compromise.

Inquiries

Questions answered.

Is Maison really printed in only black and white?
Yes. Every issue, every page, every detail. We believe color is often a shortcut around difficult design decisions. Removing it entirely forces a discipline that produces work of far greater sophistication and permanence.
How often is the print edition published?
Maison prints quarterly — four issues per year, each themed around a single principle of design. The digital journal publishes weekly dispatches between print issues to keep the conversation alive.
Can I access the full archive?
All subscribers gain access to our complete digital archive of 147 issues dating back to 2019. Patron-level members also receive the bound annual volume collecting each year's four print issues in a single archival edition.
Do you ship internationally?
We ship to 92 countries across six continents. Shipping is included in all Print and Patron subscriptions, with no hidden fees regardless of destination. Issues arrive in protective archival sleeves.
