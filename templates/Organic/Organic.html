FAQ

Questions, naturally

An accordion with organic rounded borders and smooth, breathing expand animation.

What is wabi-sabi in design?

Wabi-sabi is the acceptance of transience and imperfection. In design, it means embracing asymmetry, varied shapes, and natural textures rather than cold, mechanical precision. Every element should feel eroded by wind or shaped by hand.

Why use colored shadows instead of black?

Pure black shadows feel harsh and digital. Moss-tinted and clay-tinted shadows blend naturally with the earth palette, creating atmospheric depth that feels like dappled light through leaves.

How does the grain texture work?

A fixed pseudo-element overlays an SVG noise pattern at 4% opacity with multiply blend mode. This adds a subtle paper-like quality across the entire page without impacting performance.

Are the blob shapes customizable?

Yes. Blob border-radii use complex percentage values like 60% 40% 30% 70% / 60% 30% 70% 40%. Mix and match these to create endless organic variations—no two cards need share the same shape.
