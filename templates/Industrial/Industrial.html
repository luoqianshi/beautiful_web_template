// MANUAL

Operating manual

Neumorphic drawers with recessed arrow indicators.

What makes this different from flat design?
Industrial skeuomorphism embraces physicality. Every element exists in 3D space with consistent top-left lighting, dual neumorphic shadows, and manufacturing details (screws, vents, LEDs, scanlines). Flat design removes depth; this style celebrates it.
How do the physical interactions work?
Buttons depress with translate-y and shadow inversion (150ms). Cards elevate on hover with shadow upgrade (300ms). Icons rotate and scale. Toggles slide physically. All easing uses a mechanical spring curve: cubic-bezier(0.175, 0.885, 0.32, 1.275).
Is the safety orange required?
Yes — the safety orange (#ff4757) is the signature accent, reserved exclusively for interactive elements, status indicators, and critical alerts. It appears sparingly, like an emergency stop button. The rest of the palette stays neutral grey.
Does it stay tactile on mobile?
Absolutely. The physical metaphor persists across all breakpoints. Touch targets are minimum 48px, buttons expand to full width, and the neumorphic shadows are pure CSS (no JS) for GPU-accelerated performance.
