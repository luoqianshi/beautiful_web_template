The Establishment

A Sanctuary of Style

Conceived in 1925 by a society architect with a vision of total design, our establishment has hosted duchesses, diplomats, and the leading lights of the jazz age. Every surface is composed with intention.

The grand ballroom features a thirty-foot gilded ceiling, a sunburst marquee of hand-cut crystal, and a stage that has echoed with the horns of legends.

Membership is limited to those who appreciate that true luxury is not loud. It is the precision of a perfectly poured cocktail, the hush of velvet underfoot, the warm gleam of polished brass.

To enter is to step out of the rushing present and into an evening that moves at the unhurried pace of a more gracious age.

Inquiries

Frequently Asked

Evening attire is required. Gentlemen are asked to wear a jacket and tie; ladies, cocktail or evening dress. The establishment maintains the standards of the era it celebrates, and we thank you for honouring them.
For guest admissions, we recommend reserving no fewer than fourteen days in advance. Members enjoy priority booking up to ninety days ahead. Patron tier reservations may be made on the evening, subject to availability of the standing table.
Membership is personal and non-transferable. However, members may host up to three guests per evening, each of whom will be expected to observe the dress code and conduct of the house. Guest admission fees apply.
The grand ballroom may be reserved in its entirety for private functions on Sunday and Monday evenings. Our events director would be delighted to discuss bespoke arrangements, including custom menus, orchestra selection, and themed decoration.
