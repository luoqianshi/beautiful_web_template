03 — The System

Restrained palette.
Relentless hierarchy.

Black, white, and one accent. Maybe two. More colors dilute the typographic impact. Let the type shapes do the work.

Type as Hero

Headlines aren't labels — they're the visual centerpiece. A well-set 80pt headline is more compelling than any stock photo.

Extreme Contrast

The gap between headline and body creates drama. Think 6:1 or greater ratio between H1 and paragraph text.

Deliberate Space

Negative space isn't empty — it's the frame around your type. Generous margins make headlines feel intentional, not cramped.

Strict Hierarchy

Every element has a clear rank. No two elements compete for attention. The eye flows: headline, subhead, body, action.

Sharp Edges

No rounded corners anywhere. Sharp edges match sharp typography. Precision in every pixel.

Editorial Vibe

Confident. Editorial. Deliberate. The page feels like a luxury magazine spread where every word earns its place.

04 — The Process

Three steps to poster-grade composition.

01
Set the scale
Establish a 6:1 ratio between your headline and body. Mobile starts at text-4xl and progressively enhances to text-8xl on desktop. The drama lives in the gap.
02
Tune the tracking
Tighten display headlines to -0.06em. Widen all-caps labels to 0.2em. The letter-spacing is the personality — it signals confidence at large sizes and precision at small ones.
03
Frame with space
Let headlines bleed to the edge on mobile. Use full-width horizontal rules as dividers. Section padding grows from py-20 on mobile to py-40 on hero sections. The void is the frame.
