Section C · Benefits

Editorial authority, engineered for the web.

The newsprint aesthetic rejects modern soft-shadows and blurred backgrounds in favor of structure that commands attention. It feels like holding a fresh morning newspaper—crisp, organized, and information-rich.

  1. AAA ContrastInk black #111 on off-white #F9F9F7 exceeds 17:1 contrast ratio for optimal readability.
  2. Zero RadiusEvery component enforces sharp 90-degree corners. No exceptions, no softening.
  3. Dense GridsTight padding and collapsed borders mimic newspaper column layouts efficiently.
  4. Hard Shadows4px offset shadows on hover create a cut-out newspaper lift effect—no blur.
  5. Texture LayeringDot-grid patterns and halftone fills add depth without skeuomorphism.
  6. Timeless AuthorityEditorial design feels serious, credible, and built to outlast trends.

Why zero border radius everywhere?
Sharp 90-degree corners are the cornerstone of the newsprint aesthetic. They reinforce the editorial, authoritative feel and distinguish it from modern soft designs. The `.sharp-corners` utility enforces zero radius on every component.
How does the collapsed border system work?
Adjacent grid cells share borders to avoid double lines. Apply `border-l` and `border-t` to the container, then `border-r` and `border-b` to children. Remove the right border on the last column and bottom border on the last row.
What is the hard shadow hover effect?
Instead of soft blurred shadows, we use `box-shadow: 4px 4px 0px 0px #111111` with `translate(-2px,-2px)` on hover. This creates a "lifted" newspaper cutout effect—snappy and mechanical, never organic.
Is the design accessible?
Yes. Ink black #111111 on off-white #F9F9F7 achieves a 17:1 contrast ratio (WCAG AAA). Red #CC0000 on off-white meets AA at 5:1. Focus states use thick 2px black rings with 2px offsets.
Can I use color beyond black and white?
Editorial red (#CC0000) is used extremely sparingly—only for breaking news badges, CTAs, and hover states. 99% of the design remains pure black and white to maintain its stark authority.
