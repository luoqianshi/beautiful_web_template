★ The Process

THREE STEPS,
ZERO SUBTLETY.

1

Border it

Wrap every element in 4px of solid black. Structure is not implied — it is enforced with hard-edged lines.

2

Shadow it

Offset a solid block of ink at 45 degrees. No blur, no spread, no diffusion. Just weight and presence.

3

Rotate it

Tilt your stickers a few degrees. Break the grid on purpose. Organized chaos is the whole point.

★ FAQ

QUESTIONS,
ANSWERED LOUD.

Isn't this just ugly on purpose?+
It's "ugly-cool" — ugly by traditional polished standards, cool by rebellious intention. Neo-brutalism celebrates rawness as honesty. It requires confidence to pull off, but when it works, it's unforgettable. There's no room for timidity here.
Will it work for my serious business?+
Depends on your brand. Creative agencies, indie products, dev tools, and personal portfolios thrive with it. A bank or law firm? Maybe not. But plenty of "serious" companies use brutalism to signal they're different. The contrast is the point.
Is it accessible?+
Yes. High contrast is built in — black on cream, white on black, black on yellow all pass WCAG AA easily. We use thick focus rings and clear interactive states. The loud aesthetic doesn't sacrifice usability; it enhances visibility.
Do I need to know Tailwind?+
Nope. RAW/CO ships as plain HTML and CSS that works anywhere. No build step, no framework lock-in. Copy the components, tweak the colors, ship the site. The whole philosophy is raw and default — web 1.0 energy, modern execution.
