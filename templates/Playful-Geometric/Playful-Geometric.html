Features

A playground of components

Each sticker card connected by dashed lines, with alternating violet, pink, and yellow icon headers.

Hard Pop Shadows

Solid offset drop shadows with zero blur give every element a sticker, cut-out paper feel that begs to be clicked.

Primitive Shapes

Circles, triangles, squares, and squiggles used as backgrounds, masks, and icons. Simple, bold, and unmistakably fun.

Pattern Fills

Polka dots, grid lines, and diagonal stripes fill shapes and backgrounds with texture and energy.

Bouncy Motion

Cubic-bezier overshoot easing makes elements pop in, wiggle on hover, and feel alive. Never flat, never boring.

Varied Radii

Mixing fully rounded corners with sharp ones creates leaf shapes and asymmetric blobs. Uniformity is the enemy.

Tactile Fun

Every interaction feels physical—buttons press down, cards wiggle, badges rotate. It smiles at you.

FAQ

Curious questions

Accordion items with thick borders. When opened, the background fills with a playful dot pattern.

What is the "stable grid, wild decoration" philosophy? ›

Content (text, forms) lives in clean, readable areas, but the world around it is alive with movement and shape. We reference the Memphis Group but clean it up for modern screens—removing chaos while keeping the energy.

Why hard shadows instead of soft blur? ›

Hard, offset drop shadows (4px 4px 0px with zero blur) give elements a sticker or cut-out paper feel. Soft blurs feel corporate and sterile—hard shadows feel tactile and fun, like physical objects on a desk.

How do the bouncy animations work? ›

We use cubic-bezier(0.34, 1.56, 0.64, 1) for overshoot easing. Elements don't just fade in—they pop in with a bounce. Hover states wiggle and rotate. All motion respects prefers-reduced-motion for accessibility.

Is it accessible? ›

Yes. Slate-800 text on warm cream achieves AAA contrast. We never rely on color alone—shapes and text labels always accompany it. Focus states use thick colored borders plus hard shadows for high visibility.

Can I use this for serious products? ›

Absolutely. Playful doesn't mean unprofessional. The stable grid ensures readability and structure, while the decorations add warmth and approachability. Many education, consumer, and creative tools thrive with this aesthetic.
